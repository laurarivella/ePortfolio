Reflections

On my studies

Pursuing a Masters Degree while working full time has been a demanding endeavor which has admittedly brought me to my current limit. It has required unwavering dedication and an all in mentality to remain consistent throughout two and a half years.

As it has been during my previous academic experience as well, what one is left with, both in terms of knowledge and experience, is well worth the effort.

In a field like cybersecurity, where "experts" and "specialists" abund due to the market's demand, having a solid and well rounded foundation on which to build further will be invaluable in the years to come as I continue to pursue more knowledge.

I have been pushed while being ancouraged not to dismiss what I have learned during previous academic and professional experiences. This has led me to successfully complete the module while pursuing my own path.

On my achievements

One of the key achievements has been managing to switch work sector thanks in part to the fact that I was pursuing this degree. Upon graduating from my first Masters in Security Studies I struggled to find a job that would benefit from my sklls set and pivoting to cybersecurity offered me a great opportunity to make use of it.

Thanks to my previous academic endeavors and experiences, I would consider a figure such as myself a bridge between the technical side of cybersecurity and its human, political and contextual side.

Being able to understand both worlds has value in that it can lead to more productive cooperation and therefore more security.

On the feedback I received


It has been an absolute pleasure to participate in each and every module of the course and I have been very fortunate in that all my tutors have done a remarkable job, more often than not going above and beyond the call of duty.

The feedback process at the University of Essex has been taken extremely seriously and I have given and received feedback from my tutors regularly throughout the modules, both formally and informally.

The feedback I received both from my tutors and my peers throughout the modules and the group projects has been positive and it has encouraged me to keep smoothing out any rough edges I had and still have.

One can only self-assess so far without feedback from the outside. Both the feedback and the reflective activities have sparked a great deal of introspection and work on refining my technical, academic and social skills.
