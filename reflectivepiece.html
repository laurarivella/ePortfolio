On my studies, achievements and the feedback I received/h3>

The article gives quite a few points to reflect on since professionals working in the tech field should always keep in mind that technology affects virtually all other aspects of life from economics to the environment. (Stahl et al., 2016)

If we consider my role as a computing professional, and specifically as a cybersecurity consultant with a background in intelligence analysis, the ethical issues mentioned in the paper that affect me the most in my work are privacy, autonomy and trust.

These issues affect my role in the company in that they are the basis of whatever type of project requires my attention.

The actions and steps proposed in the analysis of autonomy, privacy and trust could lead to legal, social and professional issues. Professionally, there might not be room to implement even a single one of the actions proposed if one works for a company which is not inclined to accept them. In case of an ethical conflict which cannot be solved, resigning and distancing oneself from the situation would be ideal, even if harder to do. In terms of legal issues, one must be careful not to breach contractual agreements or undertake illegal actions, either explicitly or implicitly (e.g. by omission). From a social point of view maintaining a sound and vigilant approach would ensure both applicability of the steps and actions proposed and their positive impact on society at large through well designed tech systems and products.
