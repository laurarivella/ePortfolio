Academic endeavours

Literature Review

The literature review was carried out on cybersecurity applications in detecting political misinformation on social media platforms.

the outline was as follows: The literature review will aim to analyse the use of cybersecurity tools and techniques in detecting political misinformation on a social media platforms. Zooming in on one social media platform will become feasible once the preliminary searches will be carried out. The Introduction will address the need for the review, its significance and clarify the structure of the paper. It will also address the context, prior research, research goals. The Research methodology section will introduce the framework used to synthesise the literature, the selection criteria of the studies (together with the sources and keywords used), inclusion and exclusion criteria. In the Findings section there will be the main findings in the literature on the topic, an overview of the strengths and limitations of the selected literature. Finally, the Discussion will deal with any discrepancies in the literature and conclusions drawn from the analysis of the literature.

In the final version I expanded on Twitter as a social medial and it ended up being quite an interesting topic to investigate.
The main conclusion drawn is that for the research question examined, cybersecurity tools and efforts have been almost exclusively related or tied to AI and machine learning models to detect bots. Moving forward, greater attention should be paid to how cybersecurity ties into all aspects of misinformation on social media. Starting from the fundamental properties of cybersecurity, threat modelling can take place and an analysis of what can be done in terms of security can take place. At the same time, the pursue of hybrid approaches should be encouraged in that more subject matter experts with a mixed background of social sciences and cybersecurity should research topics such as the research question we examined. In doing so, the range of approaches to the issue would expand and contribute to making progress.
